🛡️ The Cryptographic Anomaly Paradox

This project introduces an academic simulation framework designed to evaluate the operational boundaries of modern statistical auditing tools. By establishing a payload that demonstrates near-perfect mathematical randomness, the research analyzes a vital architectural question: Can structural randomness reliably guarantee the absence of subversion? When compared to the audited transparency of TLS 1.3 and Elliptic-Curve Cryptography (ECC), this paradox highlights critical evaluation criteria for next-generation security implementations.

"If a baseline simulation can bypass industry-standard statistical validations while maintaining an alternate semantic path, automated uniformity alone cannot serve as proof of cryptographic integrity."
